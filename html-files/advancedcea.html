Advanced CEA

Cost-effectiveness of GeneXpert (MTB/RIF) assay laboratory technique compared to microscopy in Tanzania.

Abstract

Introduction

The alarming increase of multidrug resistant TB (MDR-TB) incidence and the global emergence of extensively drug resistant TB (XDR-TB), and rapid mortality due to MDR-TB or XDR-TB necessitate the development of rapid diagnostic test. After of the development of the new diagnostic tool, GeneXpert MTB/RIF assay, which can detect both TB and MDR-TB using sputum in less than two hours the WHO distributed the recommendations and guideline on the rollout of the diagnostic strategy. The Ministry of Health, United Republic of Tanzania, has planned to expand the implementation of GeneXpert MTB/RIF test in the near future; however, the paucity of supportive cost-effectiveness analysis of the diagnostic equipment within the context of the country necessitate this cost-effectiveness analysis.

Methods

We developed decision tree model to assess the cost-effectiveness of GeneXpert compared to microscopy as a Status quo. We extracted the input variables cost, transition probabilities, mortality rate, and DALYs averted from relevant secondary sources. We used cost per DALYs averted as our main outcome measure. The result of our study showed that the Incremental Cost-Effectiveness Ratio (ICER) is 32.98 US dollars per DALYs with the value of ICER ranging between ($32.32 per DALYs averted - $33.90 per DALYs averted).

Results

The findings of our cost-effectiveness study agree with several model-based economic evaluations that the full rollout GeneXpert is cost-effective. So, we strongly encourage the government of Tanzania to expedite the implementation of GeneXpert planned in 2016 to expand the utilization of the diagnostic tool across the country.
